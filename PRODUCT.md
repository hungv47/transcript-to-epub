# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Independent creators — podcasters, YouTubers, speakers, course-makers — who
already have spoken content (a talk, interview, episode they own) and want a
designed, Kindle-ready ebook out of it without doing layout work. They arrive
skeptical that "transcript → book" can look good, and they're deciding in the
first scroll whether this is a real product or a toy. The landing page IS the
pitch: it has to prove output quality before they paste anything.

Not for consumers ripping other people's videos — the product credits the
original creators and asks for an ownership attestation.

## Product Purpose

TalkToBook turns a creator's own spoken-word transcript into a calm, designed
ebook. Free preview EPUB (watermarked, plain cover); a one-time $9 unlock
removes branding and adds a custom cover/accent plus PDF and Kindle (AZW3).
Success = paid unlocks from qualified creators. The whole site exists to convert
on **output quality**, which is the engine's edge — so the design must look as
considered as the books it makes.

## Brand Personality

Calm, editorial, exact. The voice of a good book interior: quiet confidence,
nothing shouting, every element deliberate. Three words: **calm, crafted,
trustworthy.** It should feel like the finished product, not like SaaS.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-SaaS landing pages: purple/blue gradients, glassmorphism, the
  hero-metric template, identical icon-card grids.
- The over-saturated "editorial-magazine" reflex (display-italic serif + drop
  caps + broadsheet grid) — this is editorial in *restraint*, not in costume.
- Loud "convert-your-X" tool pages. We show the book; we don't shout the verb.
- Neon glow, stock photography, frosted panels (the engine's own cover prompt
  explicitly bans these — the site honors the same taste).

## Design Principles

1. **Show the book.** The strongest proof is a rendered designed page/cover on
   screen before the user does anything. Show, don't tell.
2. **Practice what you preach.** A tool that makes calm, well-set books must
   have a calm, well-set interface. The site is a sample of the work.
3. **One accent, used sparingly.** Signal-lime is a scalpel, not a paint
   roller — a rule, a keyline, a single emphasized word. Restraint with intent.
4. **Honest examples.** Real before/after, real limits stated plainly. Trust is
   the conversion lever for a skeptical creator.
5. **Quiet motion.** Movement clarifies and rewards; it never performs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast on its background; placeholders too. Full keyboard
operability with visible focus rings. Every animation has a
`prefers-reduced-motion` alternative (crossfade or instant). Decorative product
mockups are `aria-hidden`; meaning lives in the real headings and copy.
